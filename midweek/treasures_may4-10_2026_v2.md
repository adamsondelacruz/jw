# "Fully Experience Jehovah's Blessing" — Version 2
**Treasures From God's Word — 10 minutes | Isaiah 58-59**
**Week of May 4 – 10, 2026**

---

### Introduction (~1 min)

<span style="color:grey">*(warm, conversational)*</span> Picture a backyard fruit tree. <span style="color:grey">*(pause)*</span> One of those generous ones — branches sagging low with ripe mangoes, oranges, whatever fruit grows where you live. The owner planted it years ago, watered it, watched it grow. Now it's *covered* in fruit.

<span style="color:grey">*(humor)*</span> But every morning, the owner walks past that tree, gets in the car, drives to the grocery store, and buys a small bag of fruit that costs five dollars. <span style="color:grey">*(pause)*</span> Meanwhile, behind him, the tree drops perfectly ripe fruit on the ground.

<span style="color:grey">*(thoughtful)*</span> Strange? Yes. But that's exactly the picture Jehovah paints in Isaiah 58. He had planted a tree of blessings for His people. And many of them walked right past it.

The title of today's discussion is, "Fully Experience Jehovah's Blessing." Notice that little word — *fully*. The fruit is on the branch. The question is whether we'll reach up. We'll consider three ways to do exactly that.

---

### Point 1: By showing hospitality (~2.5 min)

<span style="color:grey">*(read steadily)*</span> Please turn with me to **Isaiah 58:7-9**. <span style="color:grey">*(slow delivery)*</span>

> *"It is to share your bread with the hungry, to bring the poor and homeless into your house, to clothe someone naked when you see him, and not to turn your back on your own flesh. Then your light will shine through like the dawn, and your healing will spring up quickly. Then you will call, and Jehovah will answer; you will cry for help, and he will say, 'Here I am!'"*

<span style="color:grey">*(pause)*</span> Did you catch the cause and effect? Open hands lead to open ears. We share bread — *Jehovah* says, "Here I am!"

<span style="color:grey">*(reflective)*</span> What does this teach us about *Jehovah*? He is a God who notices small kitchens. The verse doesn't say, "throw a banquet." It says, "share your bread." <span style="color:grey">*(humor, lighter tone)*</span> Even leftovers count. <span style="color:grey">*(warm)*</span> Jehovah is far more interested in the heart that opens the door than in what's on the plate.

<span style="color:grey">*(illustrative)*</span> Notice — a fruit tree doesn't keep its fruit to itself. The branches *bend down* to give it away. That is what hospitality looks like. Bending toward someone else.

*How do we draw closer to Jehovah?* Invite the new publisher over. Sit beside the elderly sister at the meeting. Make room — even small room — and Jehovah comes near.

---

### Point 2: By putting spiritual things first (~2.5 min)

<span style="color:grey">*(read with conviction)*</span> **Isaiah 58:13, 14:**

> *"If because of the Sabbath you refrain from pursuing your own interests on my holy day and you call the Sabbath an exquisite delight... then you will find your exquisite delight in Jehovah."*

<span style="color:grey">*(emphasize)*</span> Listen to that phrase: *exquisite delight*. Not "tolerable obligation." Not "Sunday duty." **Exquisite delight.**

<span style="color:grey">*(illustrative, gentle)*</span> The June 15, 2006 *Watchtower* (**w06** p. 22 ¶9) reminds us that putting spiritual things first isn't a burden Jehovah places *on* us — it's a doorway He opens *for* us.

<span style="color:grey">*(humor)*</span> Think about it this way: a fruit tree doesn't ripen on demand. You can't shake it impatiently and expect a mango on Tuesday at 4:15. <span style="color:grey">*(pause)*</span> The fruit ripens in *its* season. And it ripens *best* for the person who comes to the tree regularly — not the one who shows up every other year hoping for something sweet.

<span style="color:grey">*(slow)*</span> The Israelites? They showed up every other year. Isaiah 58 verses 1-5 — and the Insight book under "Fast" (¶3) — show that they fasted while quarreling, prayed while oppressing their workers. They went through the motions, but never really came to the tree.

*What does this teach us about Jehovah?* He isn't fooled by appearance. He sees the heart. And He longs for us to *taste* His blessing — not just check a box.

*How do we draw closer?* When the meeting clashes with overtime, when family worship competes with the next episode — choosing Jehovah first isn't sacrifice. It's coming to the tree while the fruit is ripe.

---

### Point 3: By trusting that Jehovah will care for you (~2.5 min)

<span style="color:grey">*(read with weight)*</span> **Isaiah 59:1:**

> *"Look! The hand of Jehovah is not too short to save, nor is his ear too dull to hear."*

<span style="color:grey">*(slow, deliberate)*</span> What a beautiful picture. Jehovah's hand — *not* too short. His ear — *not* too dull.

<span style="color:grey">*(personal, gentle)*</span> The January 15, 2013 *Watchtower* (**w13** p. 13 ¶5-6) reminds us that when blessings feel delayed, the problem is never on Jehovah's end. <span style="color:grey">*(humor)*</span> Jehovah doesn't have short arms. <span style="color:grey">*(pause)*</span> And He certainly doesn't need us to speak louder.

<span style="color:grey">*(illustrative, warm)*</span> Have you ever watched a child trying to pick fruit from a high branch? <span style="color:grey">*(pause)*</span> They jump. They stretch. They might even climb on a wobbly chair — <span style="color:grey">*(humor)*</span> which is a poor parenting decision waiting to happen. But then a parent walks over, reaches up effortlessly, and places the fruit *right* in the child's hand.

<span style="color:grey">*(warm)*</span> That is our Father. We do not have to reach high enough. He bends low enough.

*What does this teach us about Jehovah?* He is *able* and He is *attentive*. Two things every anxious heart needs to hear.

*How do we draw closer?* By bringing Him the worry we usually carry alone. The bill. The diagnosis. The wayward child. <span style="color:grey">*(warm)*</span> His hand is not too short. His ear is not too dull. He is *right there* — at the tree, with us.

---

### Conclusion (~1 min)

<span style="color:grey">*(sincere, building)*</span> So picture that fruit tree again. <span style="color:grey">*(pause)*</span> Branches heavy. Fruit ripe. Yours for the taking.

<span style="color:grey">*(slow delivery)*</span> Hospitality bends the branches toward others. Putting spiritual things first brings us to the tree in season. Trusting Jehovah's care lets His hand place the fruit in ours.

<span style="color:grey">*(warm, conclude)*</span> Brothers and sisters — let's not walk past the tree. Let's *fully* experience Jehovah's blessing.
