Spiritual Gems — Week of May 18–24, 2026

Bible reading: Isaiah 62-64
"What spiritual gems from this week's Bible reading would you like to share?"

Three less-common verses to choose from. Each comment is timed at ~25–30 seconds (roughly 65–75 words).

Option A — Isaiah 63:9

"During all their distress it was distressing to him. His own personal messenger saved them. In his love and compassion he repurchased them."

Comment (~28 sec):

What I really appreciated from this week's reading is Isaiah 63:9 — "During all their distress, it was distressing to him." What insight into Jehovah! When his people hurt, he hurts. He's not a distant observer. This helps me in the ministry — when I meet someone who is struggling, I can confidently tell them that Jehovah doesn't just see their tears; he feels them. That's a Father worth drawing close to.

Option B — Isaiah 64:8

"But now, O Jehovah, you are our Father. We are the clay, and you are our Potter; we are all the work of your hand."

Comment (~27 sec):

Isaiah 64:8 stood out to me: "We are the clay, and you are our Potter." What a humbling thought about Jehovah — he doesn't mass-produce his servants. He shapes each one personally and patiently. Clay can't shape itself; it just has to stay soft in the Potter's hand. In the ministry, this reminds me that growth isn't about my perfection. It's about staying willing under Jehovah's hand.

Option C — Isaiah 62:3

"You will be a beautiful crown in the hand of Jehovah, and a royal turban in the palm of your God."

Comment (~28 sec):

Isaiah 62:3 really moved me — "You will be a beautiful crown in the hand of Jehovah." Think about that. A king doesn't hide his crown in a drawer; he displays it. That's how Jehovah feels about his people. He doesn't just tolerate us — he shows us off. In the ministry, when I feel small or unnoticed, this reminds me: to Jehovah, faithful publishers aren't a burden. We are his crown.
