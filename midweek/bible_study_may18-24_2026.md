# Lesson 86 — Jesus Raises Lazarus

## Comment 86-1 — Jesus wept

> *"Jesus gave way to **tears**."* — John 11:35

**Comment (~29 sec):**

What stood out to me about Jesus is John 11:35 — *"Jesus gave way to tears."* He knew he was going to raise Lazarus moments later. He didn't *need* to cry. But seeing Mary's grief moved him. This teaches me that even when Jehovah has the solution in his hands, he doesn't dismiss our pain. In the ministry, this helps me sit with people in their suffering rather than rushing them past it.

---

## Comment 86-2 — Jesus delayed two days

> *"When he heard that Lazarus was sick, he then actually **remained two more days** in the place where he was."* — John 11:6

**Comment (~28 sec):**

What I appreciated is that Jesus deliberately waited two days before going to Lazarus (John 11:6). From a human view, this seems wrong — his friend is dying! But Jesus' delay set the stage for a much greater miracle. This teaches me about Jehovah's **timing** — what feels like silence is often setup. In the ministry, when honest-hearted ones seem slow to respond, I can trust Jehovah's timing rather than my own.

---

## Comment 86-3 — "I am the resurrection and the life"

> *"I am the **resurrection** and the **life**. The one who exercises faith in me, even though he dies, will come to life."* — John 11:25

**Comment (~27 sec):**

What moved me is Jesus' words to Martha — *"I am the resurrection and the life."* Martha was looking ahead to a future hope. Jesus essentially said, *"Look at me — the future hope is standing in front of you."* What I learn is that Jehovah's promises aren't abstract; they're embodied. In the ministry, this gives me confidence: I'm offering people a real person, not just a doctrine.

---

## Comment 86-4 — "Loose him and let him go"

> *"Jesus said to them: '**Loose him and let him go**.'"* — John 11:44

**Comment (~30 sec):**

What I noticed is that Jesus raised Lazarus, but then said to others, *"Loose him and let him go."* Jesus did the impossible part — but he involved others in the practical part. What this teaches me about Jehovah is that he invites our cooperation in his work, even though he doesn't need us. In the ministry, that's exactly our role: Jehovah resurrects hearts; we just help **unwrap** the grave clothes.

# Lesson 87 — Jesus' Last Supper

## Comment 87-1 — Simple bread and wine

> *"Keep doing this in **remembrance** of me."* — Luke 22:19

**Comment (~29 sec):**

What I appreciated about Jesus' last supper is how *simple* he kept it — bread, wine, and the words *"do this in remembrance of me."* He didn't ask for monuments or rituals. Just **remembrance**. What this teaches me about Jehovah is that he values *meaning* over spectacle. In the ministry, this helps me explain the Memorial: it's not pageantry — it's one of the most personal invitations Jehovah extends to humans.

---

## Comment 87-2 — Greatest is the youngest

> *"Let the one who is greatest among you become as the **youngest**, and the one taking the lead as the one **ministering**."* — Luke 22:26

**Comment (~28 sec):**

What surprised me is that even at the *last* supper, the apostles argued about who was greatest. Jesus didn't scold — he served. He said the greatest is *"the one who sees himself as the youngest."* What I learn about Jehovah is that greatness in his eyes flips the world's measuring stick. In the ministry, this reminds me: the brother who sets up chairs is closer to Jesus than the one chasing prominence.

---

## Comment 87-3 — Love as identifying mark

> *"By this all will know that you are my **disciples** — if you have **love** among yourselves."* — John 13:35

**Comment (~29 sec):**

What stood out is Jesus' words in John 13:35: *"By this all will know that you are my disciples — if you have love among yourselves."* Notice he didn't say *doctrine* would identify us. Or *signs*. Just **love**. What I learn about Jehovah is that he made love the unmistakable trademark of his people. In the ministry, how I treat my brothers matters as much as the message I share.

---

## Comment 87-4 — Jesus calls them friends

> *"I have called you **friends**, because I have made known to you all the things I have heard from my Father."* — John 15:15

**Comment (~28 sec):**

What touched me is John 15:15, where Jesus calls his apostles *friends* — *"because I have made known to you all the things I have heard from my Father."* Friendship, in Jesus' definition, includes **transparency**. What I learn about Jehovah is that he wants a relationship that *shares*, not a distant one. In the ministry, this is what we offer people — not just truth, but friendship with Jehovah himself.
