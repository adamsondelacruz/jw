# "Fully Experience Jehovah's Blessing"
**Treasures From God's Word — 10 minutes | Isaiah 58-59**
**Week of May 4 – 10, 2026**

---

### Introduction (~1 min)

<span style="color:grey">*(warm, conversational)*</span> Imagine you go to a friend's house for dinner. You walk in, and there's a beautiful table — candles lit, plates set, a roast in the middle that smells *incredible*. <span style="color:grey">*(humor)*</span> You sit down, ready to dig in... and your friend hands you a *photograph* of the meal. "Enjoy!" he says.

<span style="color:grey">*(thoughtful pause)*</span> That's a strange picture, isn't it? Yet that is exactly what life was like for many Israelites in Isaiah's day. Jehovah had spread out a feast of blessings — and they walked away with the photograph instead of the meal.

The title of today's discussion is, "Fully Experience Jehovah's Blessing." Notice that little word — *fully*. Jehovah doesn't want us holding the photograph. He wants us at the table. We'll consider three ways we can fully experience His blessing.

---

### Point 1: By showing hospitality (~2.5 min)

<span style="color:grey">*(read steadily)*</span> Please turn with me to **Isaiah 58:7-9**. <span style="color:grey">*(slow delivery)*</span>

> *"It is to share your bread with the hungry, to bring the poor and homeless into your house, to clothe someone naked when you see him, and not to turn your back on your own flesh. Then your light will shine through like the dawn, and your healing will spring up quickly. Then you will call, and Jehovah will answer; you will cry for help, and he will say, 'Here I am!'"*

<span style="color:grey">*(pause)*</span> Did you notice the cause and effect? Jehovah ties open hands to open ears. We share bread — *He* says, "Here I am!"

<span style="color:grey">*(reflective)*</span> What does this teach us about *Jehovah*? He is a God who notices small kitchens. The verse doesn't say "throw a banquet." It says "share your bread." <span style="color:grey">*(humor, lighter tone)*</span> Even if all you have is leftovers — Jehovah notices the leftovers. <span style="color:grey">*(warm)*</span> He cares less about the menu and more about the heart that opens the door.

<span style="color:grey">*(personal)*</span> Think of a houseplant on a sunny windowsill. The plant doesn't make the light — it just turns *toward* it. Hospitality is how we turn toward Jehovah's light. *And then* — verse 8 says — that light shines *through* us.

*How do we draw closer to Jehovah?* Invite the new publisher over. Sit with the elderly sister at the meeting. Make room — even small room — and Jehovah moves closer.

---

### Point 2: By putting spiritual things first (~2.5 min)

<span style="color:grey">*(read with conviction)*</span> **Isaiah 58:13, 14:**

> *"If because of the Sabbath you refrain from pursuing your own interests on my holy day and you call the Sabbath an exquisite delight... then you will find your exquisite delight in Jehovah."*

<span style="color:grey">*(emphasize)*</span> Listen to that phrase: *exquisite delight*. Not "tolerable obligation." Not "Sunday duty." **Exquisite delight.**

<span style="color:grey">*(illustrative, gentle)*</span> The June 15, 2006 *Watchtower* (**w06** p. 22 ¶9) reminds us that putting spiritual things first isn't a burden Jehovah places *on* us — it's a doorway He opens *for* us. <span style="color:grey">*(humor)*</span> It's the difference between being told to eat broccoli and being handed a slice of perfectly ripe mango. <span style="color:grey">*(pause)*</span> One you endure. The other you *delight* in.

<span style="color:grey">*(slow)*</span> The Israelites went through the motions. Isaiah 58 verses 1-5 — and the Insight book under "Fast" (¶3) — show that they fasted while quarreling, prayed while oppressing workers. They held the photograph. They missed the meal.

*What does this teach us about Jehovah?* He isn't fooled by appearance. He sees the heart. And He longs for us to *taste* His blessing — not just check a box.

*How do we draw closer?* When the meeting clashes with overtime, when family worship competes with the next episode — choosing Jehovah first isn't sacrifice. It's accepting His invitation to the table.

---

### Point 3: By trusting that Jehovah will care for you (~2.5 min)

<span style="color:grey">*(read with weight)*</span> **Isaiah 59:1:**

> *"Look! The hand of Jehovah is not too short to save, nor is his ear too dull to hear."*

<span style="color:grey">*(slow, deliberate)*</span> What a beautiful picture. Jehovah's hand — *not* too short. His ear — *not* too dull.

<span style="color:grey">*(personal, gentle)*</span> The January 15, 2013 *Watchtower* (**w13** p. 13 ¶5-6) reminds us that when blessings feel delayed, the problem is never on Jehovah's end. It's never that He couldn't reach. <span style="color:grey">*(humor)*</span> Jehovah doesn't have short arms. <span style="color:grey">*(pause)*</span> And He certainly doesn't need us to speak louder.

<span style="color:grey">*(illustrative, warm)*</span> Picture a small child reaching up at the dinner table. The father doesn't measure how high the child can stretch — he simply leans down and lifts the child up. That is our God.

*What does this teach us about Jehovah?* He is *able* and He is *attentive*. Two things every anxious heart needs to hear.

*How do we draw closer?* By bringing Him the worry we usually carry alone. The bill. The diagnosis. The wayward child. <span style="color:grey">*(warm)*</span> His hand is not too short. His ear is not too dull. He is *right there*.

---

### Conclusion (~1 min)

<span style="color:grey">*(sincere, building)*</span> So picture the table again. The candles are lit. The food is hot. <span style="color:grey">*(pause)*</span> Jehovah has set every place for His people.

<span style="color:grey">*(slow delivery)*</span> Hospitality opens the door. Putting spiritual things first pulls out the chair. Trusting His care lifts the fork.

<span style="color:grey">*(warm, conclude)*</span> Brothers and sisters — let's not settle for the photograph. Let's *fully* experience Jehovah's blessing.

---

### Delivery notes
- Total: ~9.5 min spoken, leaving 30 sec buffer
- Three scripture readings ≈ 50 sec total
- Three humor beats (photograph meal, leftovers, Jehovah's arms aren't short) — keep light
- Recurring "table" image bookends the talk; mango/houseplant/lifted child as nature/food illustrations
