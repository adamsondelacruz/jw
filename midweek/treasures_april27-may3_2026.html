"We Are Happy to Have Jehovah as Our God"

Treasures From God's Word — 10 minutes | Isaiah 56-57
Week of April 27 – May 3, 2026

Introduction (~1 min)

(warm, conversational) Have you ever watched someone try to sit on a beach chair right at the water's edge? (humor) They get settled, drink in hand, eyes closed... and then a wave knocks the chair sideways. They scoot back. Settle in again. Another wave. Sand everywhere. Drink gone.

(thoughtful pause) That picture — someone who just cannot find rest no matter how they reposition themselves — is exactly the picture Jehovah paints in Isaiah chapter 57. Today we'll see why being on Jehovah's side isn't just the right place to be — it's the only place where we can actually rest.

We'll consider three reasons we are happy to have Jehovah as our God.

Point 1: Idol worshippers who cry out for help are not heard (~2.5 min)

(read with conviction) Please open your Bibles to Isaiah 57:13. (slow delivery)

"When you cry for help, your collection of idols will not rescue you. A wind will carry all of them away..."

(pause) Notice the contrast Jehovah draws. People had built up a whole collection of idols — they invested time, money, hope. But when trouble came and they cried out, the idols were so weightless that a breeze carried them off.

(reflective) The Isaiah's Prophecy book (ip-2 p. 269 ¶14) helps us see this isn't just about ancient statues. Anything we trust to rescue us instead of Jehovah is an idol. Career security. A bank balance. Even our own cleverness. (humor, lighter tone) Some of us put more faith in our morning coffee than in prayer — and brothers, I include myself in that.

(warm) But here's the beautiful thing — Jehovah never has to be carried by the wind. (slow) He IS the One who sends the wind.

How does this draw us closer to Him? When we feel that pull to lean on something visible, something we can hold — that's the moment to pause and cry out to Jehovah instead. Every time we do, we strengthen the bond.

Point 2: People without Jehovah are restless and divided (~2.5 min)

(read steadily) Isaiah 57:20:

"But the wicked are like the restless sea that cannot calm down, and its waters keep tossing up seaweed and mire."

(illustrative, vivid) Have you ever stood at the shore after a storm? The water isn't beautiful blue — it's churning brown, throwing up tangled weeds, dead fish, old plastic bottles. (pause) That's the picture Jehovah uses for a life without Him. Not just unhappy — but constantly bringing the worst things back to the surface.

The Insight book under "Earth" (¶21) and the June 2018 Watchtower (w18.06 p. 7 ¶16) point out that this restlessness is why the world feels so divided today. People without Jehovah aren't just personally restless — they pull others into the churn with them.

(personal, gentle) Think about your most peaceful moments this week. (pause) Was it scrolling the news? (humor) I'm guessing not. It was probably reading a scripture, singing a Kingdom song, talking with a brother or sister. Jehovah's people aren't a stagnant pond — but we're not a tossing sea either. We're like a steady river — moving, but with direction.

How does this draw us closer to Him? Every time we choose Jehovah's calm over the world's churn — by what we watch, who we associate with, how we spend a Saturday morning — we are choosing the shore over the storm.

Point 3: There is no peace for the wicked (~2.5 min)

(read with weight) Isaiah 57:21:

"'There is no peace,' says my God, 'for the wicked.'"

(slow, deliberate) Eight words. No exceptions. No fine print.

The Insight book under "Peace" (¶3) explains that the Hebrew word for peace — shalom — doesn't just mean absence of conflict. It means completeness, wholeness, soundness, welfare. (emphasize) It's the feeling you get biting into perfectly ripe fruit — nothing missing, nothing extra. (humor) Not the sad grocery-store peach that looks great and tastes like wet cardboard. (pause) I mean a peach that tastes like a peach.

(warm) That's the kind of peace Jehovah offers — not artificial, not partial, but complete.

How does this draw us closer to Him? When we ask ourselves Jehovah's question — "How does a good relationship with Jehovah make my life better?" — the answer isn't just "I avoid bad things." The answer is, "I get to taste real shalom — wholeness — that no one outside the truth even knows exists."

Conclusion (~1 min)

(sincere, building) So picture again that person on the beach chair, getting battered by waves. (pause) Now picture someone else — sitting back from the shore, on solid ground, watching the same sea, completely at rest.

(slow delivery) Same world. Same waves. Different relationship with the One who made the sea.

(warm, conclude) Brothers and sisters — we are the ones on solid ground. And that is why we can say, with our whole heart: we are happy to have Jehovah as our God.

Delivery notes

  • Total: ~9.5 min spoken, leaving 30 sec buffer
  • Three scripture readings ≈ 45 sec total
  • Two humor beats (coffee, peach) — keep light, don't oversell
  • Two nature illustrations (beach chair frame, ripe fruit) bookend the talk
