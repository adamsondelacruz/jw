## Option A — Isaiah 63:9

> *"During all their **distress** it was **distressing to him**. His own personal messenger saved them. In his love and compassion he repurchased them."*

**Comment (~28 sec):**

What I really appreciated from this week's reading is Isaiah 63:9 — *"During all their distress, it was distressing to him."* What insight into Jehovah! When his people hurt, **he** hurts. He's not a distant observer. This helps me in the ministry — when I meet someone who is struggling, I can confidently tell them that Jehovah doesn't just see their tears; he feels them. That's a Father worth drawing close to.

---

## Option B — Isaiah 64:8

> *"But now, O Jehovah, you are our Father. We are the **clay**, and you are our **Potter**; we are all the work of your hand."*

**Comment (~27 sec):**

Isaiah 64:8 stood out to me: *"We are the clay, and you are our Potter."* What a humbling thought about Jehovah — he doesn't mass-produce his servants. He shapes each one personally and patiently. Clay can't shape itself; it just has to stay soft in the Potter's hand. In the ministry, this reminds me that growth isn't about *my* perfection. It's about staying willing under Jehovah's hand.

---

## Option C — Isaiah 62:3

> *"You will be a beautiful **crown** in the hand of Jehovah, and a royal turban in the palm of your God."*

**Comment (~28 sec):**

Isaiah 62:3 really moved me — *"You will be a beautiful crown in the hand of Jehovah."* Think about that. A king doesn't hide his crown in a drawer; he displays it. That's how Jehovah feels about his people. He doesn't just tolerate us — he **shows us off**. In the ministry, when I feel small or unnoticed, this reminds me: to Jehovah, faithful publishers aren't a burden. We are his crown.

---

## Option D — Isaiah 62:4

> *"No longer will you be called a woman entirely abandoned... but you yourself will be called **My Delight Is in Her**, and your land Owned as a Wife."*

**Comment (~28 sec):**

Isaiah 62:4 caught me because Jehovah literally gives his people a new *name*: **"My Delight Is in Her."** That's not a title — that's how Jehovah thinks of them. What this teaches me is that Jehovah doesn't define us by our past; he defines us by his affection. In the ministry, this helps me reach people who feel rejected or forgotten — Jehovah is ready to give them a new name too.

---

## Option E — Isaiah 62:5

> *"As a young man marries a virgin... and as a **bridegroom** **rejoices** over his bride, so will your God rejoice over you."*

**Comment (~29 sec):**

Isaiah 62:5 is one of the warmest verses in the chapter: *"As a bridegroom rejoices over his bride, so will your God rejoice over you."* Think about that — Jehovah doesn't just *tolerate* his people; he **rejoices** over them like a bridegroom. That changes how I see my own service. In the ministry, I can confidently tell people: there is Someone who delights in you, even now, even today.

---

## Option F — Isaiah 62:6, 7

> *"All day and all night they must never be **silent**. You who are making mention of Jehovah, do not let silence come upon yourselves; and **give him no rest** until he firmly establishes Jerusalem..."*

**Comment (~27 sec):**

Isaiah 62:6, 7 surprised me — Jehovah tells his watchmen to **"give him no rest"** until his purpose is fulfilled. What this teaches me about Jehovah is that he actually *invites* our persistent prayers. He's not annoyed by them. In the ministry, this gives me courage to keep praying — for that hard call, that estranged relative, that interested person — knowing Jehovah welcomes the persistence.

---

## Option G — Isaiah 63:7

> *"I will **mention** Jehovah's expressions of **loyal love**, Jehovah's praiseworthy acts, because of all that Jehovah has done for us..."*

**Comment (~29 sec):**

Isaiah 63:7 is a verse I want to imitate: *"I will mention Jehovah's expressions of loyal love."* Notice — Isaiah doesn't just *feel* grateful, he **speaks** it out loud. What I learn is that Jehovah treasures verbal appreciation. He's not just looking at our hearts; he loves to *hear* our words. In the ministry, this reminds me to spend less time complaining and more time mentioning his loyal love out loud.

---

## Option H — Isaiah 63:16

> *"For you are our **Father**; though Abraham did not know us and Israel does not recognize us, you, O Jehovah, are our Father. Our **Repurchaser** of long ago is your name."*

**Comment (~29 sec):**

Isaiah 63:16 contains a powerful line: *"Though Abraham did not know us... you, O Jehovah, are our Father."* What this teaches me is that even when no human knows us — even when family disowns us — Jehovah remains **Father and Repurchaser**. In the ministry, this is a lifeline I can offer to someone who has been disowned, abandoned, or estranged. Jehovah's fatherhood doesn't depend on anyone else's recognition.

---

## Option I — Isaiah 64:4

> *"From of old no one has heard or listened, no eye has seen any God except you, who **acts** for the one who **keeps hoping** in him."*

**Comment (~30 sec):**

Isaiah 64:4 makes a striking claim: Jehovah *"acts for the one who keeps hoping in him."* Notice — not who acts most cleverly, but who **keeps hoping**. What I learn about Jehovah is that he rewards patience, not just performance. In the ministry, this helps me encourage someone whose Bible study seems slow, or who has been waiting years for a family member to accept the truth. Hope is what Jehovah responds to.

---

## Option J — Isaiah 62:10

> *"Pass out, pass out through the gates! **Clear the way** for the people! Build up, build up the highway! Clear away the **stones**! Raise up a signal for the peoples!"*

**Comment (~29 sec):**

Isaiah 62:10 caught me with its urgency: *"Pass out through the gates! Clear the way for the people!"* What I love is that Jehovah doesn't just *invite* people in — he wants the **road** cleared so they can come. In the ministry, that's our work. We're not just announcing the Kingdom; we're removing obstacles, answering objections, making the path easier for honest-hearted ones to walk. Jehovah cares about the road.
