Talk No. 33 — Will We Ever See True Justice?
Total time: 30 minutes
Read scriptures: Ecclesiastes 4:1  |  Isaiah 9:6, 7  |  1 Peter 5:6, 7  |  Jeremiah 10:23  |  Galatians 5:22, 23  |  Romans 15:13

Opening — Weeds Illustration (~1 min)

Have you ever tried clearing weeds in a garden?

You cut them... and for a while, everything looks clean.

But after some time... they come back. Sometimes even thicker.

Why? Because the roots are still there.

And that helps us understand something important. For thousands of years, humans have tried to fix injustice. But it keeps coming back.

Why? Because the root cause is still there.

So this raises an important question — Will we ever see true justice?

We Live in a World Filled With Injustice (3 min)

Mankind has a long history of injustice. Notice what the Bible says.

Read Ecclesiastes 4:1

Those words were written about 3,000 years ago — and yet they perfectly describe our world today.

Injustice, prejudice, abuse, and oppression continue to abound in our day. (Ecclesiastes 8:9)

Have you ever heard children playing... and suddenly one cries out: "That's not fair!"

Even young children instinctively feel when something is unjust. In a way, that simple cry has echoed throughout human history. Because as we grow older, we begin to see something more serious — it's not just small situations that are unfair. The world itself often feels unfair.

And many people today carry that same feeling — especially those who have personally suffered injustice.

If you have suffered injustice, you no doubt yearn for relief and comfort.

Is Rebellion the Answer? (4 min)

It is natural to want to seek relief from oppression. (Job 35:9)

Some rebel, trying to lessen injustice.

As Albert Einstein once observed:

"We cannot solve our problems with the same thinking we used when we created them."

If that is true — can humans really fix injustice on their own? Is rebellion the answer?

The Bible is honest about this. Rebellion does not correct the root cause of injustice. (Ecclesiastes 1:15) — "What is crooked cannot be made straight."

It's like our illustration of the weeds. You can keep cutting them down, but if you never remove the roots, the problem keeps coming back. Sometimes it even grows back worse.

So what is the root cause?

Injustice is caused by people failing to follow God's just and righteous ways. (Deuteronomy 32:4, 5)

And the Bible is clear — mankind's attempts to bring about true justice apart from God are doomed to fail. (Psalm 146:3)

Read Jeremiah 10:23

Humans simply do not have the ability to direct their own steps successfully.

So if rebellion is not the answer — what is?

What Is God's Solution to Injustice? (12 min)

God has not left us without a solution. He has established a heavenly Kingdom that will bring complete justice. (Daniel 7:13, 14)

This Kingdom will soon replace human governments and rule over the entire earth. And its Ruler is someone uniquely qualified.

Read Isaiah 9:6, 7

Jesus Christ is the Ruler of God's Kingdom.

Notice that his rule brings about "justice and righteousness" — and it will have no end. (Isaiah 32:1)

Now, during his earthly ministry, Jesus did not promote social or political reforms. He did not lead a rebellion.

Instead, he taught his followers to put faith in the Kingdom. (Matthew 4:23; 6:9, 10)

And his miracles were not just acts of compassion — they were previews.

They showed what he would do as King of God's Kingdom.

He fed the hungry. (Matthew 14:17-21)

He healed the sick. (Luke 18:35-43)

He demonstrated on a small scale what the Kingdom will accomplish globally.

Under the Kingdom's rule, injustice, prejudice, abuse, and oppression will be eliminated. (Psalm 72:12-14)

Think about what that means practically:

Everyone will share earth's resources, eliminating economic injustice. (Psalm 72:16)

All races and ethnicities will be treated equally. (Acts 10:34, 35)

Religious differences will be removed as all are taught to worship Jehovah unitedly. (Isaiah 11:9)

The home and family will be havens of peace.

God doesn't just trim the problem — he removes it at the root. Every form of injustice that has ever caused pain will be gone — permanently.

How Can You Cope With Injustice Now? (11 min)

But what about right now? While we wait for the Kingdom to act, can anything be done?

Yes. You may be able to find relief from some forms of injustice or mistreatment even now.

Be wise and cautious.

Learn to recognize and, if possible, avoid situations in which mistreatment is likely. (Proverbs 22:3)

Use available legal means.

If you have been victimized, it is not wrong to seek justice through the legal system. (Romans 13:4)

Turn to Jehovah in prayer.

If you are denied justice, pour out your feelings to Jehovah God in prayer.

Read 1 Peter 5:6, 7

He invites us to throw all our anxiety on him. He will comfort and strengthen you. (Romans 12:12)

And at his chosen time, he will punish the wicked. (Romans 12:19)

Develop the fruitage of the spirit.

Read Galatians 5:22, 23

There is a powerful truth worth reflecting on:

"Suffering feels unbearable when it has no meaning... but when a person understands why, he can endure far more."

And that is exactly what the Bible gives us — understanding.

We understand why injustice exists.

We understand why it continues.

And we understand how God will fix it.

That understanding changes how we endure. We may not be able to remove the injustice now — but we can understand it, and that changes everything.

Instead of reacting with anger — we can show patience.

Instead of returning harm — we can show kindness.

Instead of losing control — we exercise self-control.

These qualities help us remain calm in the face of injustice and avoid making matters worse. (Romans 12:17)

In fact, we may even soften the heart of oppressors and reduce their mistreatment. (Romans 12:20, 21)

And we can find genuine joy and peace right now, despite injustice.

Read Romans 15:13

Conclusion — Return to Weeds (~1 min)

Let's go back to our illustration.

Imagine removing the weeds — roots and all.

Now what happens?

The garden doesn't just look good for a few days. It stays clean. Healthy plants can grow. Everything thrives.

That is what God's Kingdom will do. Not just improve things temporarily. Not just reduce injustice.

It will remove it completely — right down to the root.

So yes — we will see true justice.

So build your faith in God's Kingdom — because true justice is not just a hope. It is a promise. It is at the heart of the sanctification of his name — Jehovah — which means "He Causes to Become." What he has purposed, he will make a reality.
